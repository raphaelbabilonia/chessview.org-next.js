# ChessView Data And AI Asset Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-03

The source code in this repository is open source. ChessView data, production information, and AI/analytics assets are not open source unless ChessView publishes them under a separate written data license.

## What The AGPL Covers

The AGPL covers the source code in this repository, unless a file says otherwise.

The AGPL does not grant rights to production databases, private datasets, user data, analytics stores, AI assets, commercial service data, or third-party content.

## Reserved ChessView Data

The following are proprietary to ChessView or controlled under separate agreements:

- Production database contents.
- User accounts, registrations, organizer records, and private player data.
- Curated tournament and news records.
- Source registries and source-quality metadata.
- Scraped, fetched, normalized, deduplicated, approved, rejected, or reviewed records.
- Evidence files, run manifests, state files, logs, and dedupe indices.
- AI search indexes, embeddings, vector databases, ranking signals, recommendation datasets, and analytics databases.
- Commercial prompts, evaluation sets, moderation rules, enrichment rules, and data pipelines used for paid services.
- Uploaded files, private documents, operational metrics, and private API data.

## Public Website Data

ChessView may display selected event or news metadata publicly so users can discover tournaments and sources.

Unless a separate written license says otherwise, public display does not grant permission to bulk copy, scrape, mirror, resell, redistribute, train models on, or build a competing database from ChessView data.

Some factual information may not be protectable by copyright in every jurisdiction. ChessView still reserves rights in database selection, curation, verification, presentation, access controls, contracts, and database rights where available.

## Third-Party Source Material

ChessView may link to tournament organizers, federations, publishers, and other source websites. Third-party content remains subject to the rights and terms of those third parties.

ChessView does not grant rights to copy third-party articles, regulations, PDFs, images, logos, or databases.

## User And Organizer Content

Users and organizers are responsible for having the rights needed to submit content to ChessView.

By submitting content to the official ChessView service, users grant ChessView the rights needed to host, process, display, index, moderate, back up, and improve the service, including related search and analytics features.

Private personal data remains subject to the ChessView privacy policy and applicable law.
